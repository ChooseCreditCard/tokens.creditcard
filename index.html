Tokens in Card Payments

From network tokens to on-chain rewards and virtual card numbers.

What is a token?

In payments, a token is a stand-in for your real card number or account. It reduces the exposure of sensitive data and can be limited to a device, merchant or channel.

  • • Network tokens (Visa/MC tokenization services)
  • • Device tokens in mobile wallets
  • • Virtual card numbers for online use
  • • On-chain tokens used in loyalty or cashback

Main token types in cards

Network token

Replaces the PAN at the scheme level. Can be locked to a device or merchant.

Device token

Unique token stored in Apple Pay/Google Pay or wearables, tied to that device.

Virtual card

A separate card number (token) for online purchases or subscriptions.

Loyalty token

Points or cashback represented as digital tokens or on-chain balances.

Why tokens matter

  • • Lower risk if one token is compromised
  • • Easier lifecycle management (rotate tokens instead of card)
  • • Better device and merchant binding
  • • Stronger foundations for Web3-style loyalty and identity

Tokens in crypto-linked cards

Crypto-linked cards often combine traditional payment tokens with blockchain tokens:

  • • Network tokens for card transactions at POS
  • • Blockchain tokens for rewards or governance
  • • Stablecoins to bridge value onto card rails
  • • Smart contracts to automate loyalty or tiers
